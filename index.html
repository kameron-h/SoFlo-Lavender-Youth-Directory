    • About My Topic

    Florida has experienced a massive influx of anti-LGBTQ+ legislation in recent years, especially regarding the exposure of LGBTQ+ topics to youth. Examples of Florida's anti-LGBTQ+ legislation include the "Don't Say Gay" bill, transgender youth sports bans, "Drag Queen Storytime" bans, and multiple bills that have severely restricted access to gender-affirming care.

    Young people in the LGBTQ+ community report instances of discrimination and violence at significantly higher rates than their straight and cisgender peers. Due to Florida's censorship laws that keep LGBTQ+ topics away from children, LGBTQ+ youth are barred from resources and community support that can save their lives. That is why this directory was created; so that LGBTQ+ youth (and allies) can find all of South FL's LGBTQ+ resources in one place.

    Data about my topic

    Charts:

    This chart shows rates of discrimination experienced by LGBQ+ youth, categorized by sexuality (Gay, Lesbian, Bisexual, Pansexual, Queer, Questioning, Asexual).

    In every category, over 50% of survey takers reported discrimination based on their sexual orientation. The highest percentages were 73% and 72% of gay and lesbian youth respectively.


    This chart shows rates of discrimination experienced by transgender and nonbinary youth, categorized by gender identity (Transgender boy/man, Transgender girl/woman, Nonbinary/genderqueer).

    Once again, over 50% of survey takers in each category reported discrimination based on their gender identity. The highest percentages were 86% of transgender boys/men and 77% of transgender girls/women.


    This chart shows how community acceptance of LGBTQ+ people impacts the suicide attempt rate of LGBTQ+ youth, categorized into four levels (Very Unaccepting, Somewhat Unaccepting, Somewhat Accepting, Very Accepting).

    The data shows an obvious correlation between the acceptance level and suicide risk. In communities that are very unaccepting, such as Florida, the suicide attempt rate of LGBTQ+ youth is highest. This is why it is so crucial to spread awareness about LGBTQ+ resources to youth in South FL.

    Statistics:

    • 45% of LGBTQ+ youth seriously considered suicide in 2022 (Source).
    • 93% of transgender & nonbinary youth said they were worried about being denied access to gender-affirming care due to state or local laws (Source).
    • Nearly 1 in 4 LGBQ+ teens have experienced sexual violence compared to 1 in 12 heterosexual teens (Source).
    • Transgender people are over 4 times more likely than cisgender people to experience violent victimization (Source).
